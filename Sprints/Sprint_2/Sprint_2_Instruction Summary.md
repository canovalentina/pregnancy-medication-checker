# Sprint #2 Project Planning 
[Format: JDF- submit a pdf file]

1.	Establish the topic officially with mentor
2.	Start on Sprint: consists of;
   
a.	supplemental research, 

b.	technical design, 

c.	and an implementation plan (tasks and timeline).

# The submission includes:
## Part 1 - Project Topic (around 1 page, up to 2 pages.)

a brief introduction to your project in alignment with your mentor expectations

### a.	Background

A short background on the project topic.

consist of major or foundational research, general descriptions of the problem you will be addressing.

### b.	Justification

illustrate why your project matters.

### c.	Solution

high level narrative description of your solution.

Describe what you are building or the research you are undertaking.

This solution should be a logical approach from your background and justification.

## Part 2 - Technical Design (narrative/list portions should be fairly short (under a page). May include images and diagrams.)

lay out the preliminary technical design for your project.

### a.	Tools/Technology

simple list of any tools/technology you will be using:

programming languages and frameworks, hosting services or other delivery methods,

as bulleted list.

### b.	Datasets and Data Sources

### c.	Architecture Diagram

simple architecture diagram of your solution.

### d.	Screen Mock-up(s)

One is enough

## Part 3 - Implementation Plan

### a.	Project tasks

a list of high level tasks to be executed in order to complete your project.

tasks titles should be self-explanatory in the context of your project.

Include the project sprints on this to go with your timeline.

(You are free to lay these out in a Kanban board or any tool that can track tasking (e.g. Jira, GitHub Projects, etc.) and provide a screenshot or link.)

### b.	Project Timeline

Provide a timeline to go with each task, as start date and end date. (break it down by week)

(You may combine the project timeline and task lists into a single table.)

### c.	Needs/Risks

can be major dependencies or areas of vulnerability that would disrupt the project if not addressed.

risk would also include anything that which may be a potential failure.

(You may include this in a table alongside the tasks and timeline.)
